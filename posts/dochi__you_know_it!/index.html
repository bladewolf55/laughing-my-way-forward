Dochi? You Know It!

2023-03-05 18:54

This'll be a short entry. I didn't sleep so well last night, not due to depression but instead my belly aching--and not the slang kind.

It's a chronic thing I won't dwell on in this post. All was soothed when my wife took me out for dochi.


Another Believer, CC BY-SA 4.0 https://creativecommons.org/licenses/by-sa/4.0, via Wikimedia Commons

She had salted caramel. I had a matcha and a cinnamon crunch.

Was it world shattering, the microsecond man-made, more-energy-out-than-in fusion reaction that proves the sun isn't the only cosmic energy game in town?

I wouldn't go that far. It was an iced donut with a slightly chewy coating that was somehow satisfying. On the other hand, unlike nuclear fusion, I could hold it in my hand and feed bits to my wife. The latter drew an approving smile from the father of a family who were clearly there to pick up a big box of the things. At one point I wasn't sure if their filled box would survive the thirteen feet to the exit without being emptied. (More by parents than kids, I will not comment.)

The dochi were delightful. We didn't set out for downtown this morning in search of euphoria. Just a modicum of happy, a slice of grinny, a palate-pleasing, portmanteau pastry.

It worked. And the iced Vietnamese coffee wasn't bad, either. Cảm ơn!
